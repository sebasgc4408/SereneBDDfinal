# Product Guidelines: [Name TBD]

## 1. Voice and Tone
### 1.1 Core Principles
- **Supportive & Encouraging:** The product should act as a calm companion. Use phrases like "You're all set," "Everything is running smoothly," and "We'll handle the details" to ensure the psychologist feels accompanied.
- **Precise & Transparent:** Trust is built through clarity. Every permission and action must be explained in plain human language (e.g., "This allows patients to see your available hours without seeing your private events").
- **Minimalist & Quiet:** Avoid "noisy" or "salesy" language. Use as few words as possible and eliminate exclamation points or aggressive CTAs.
- **Identity:** Reassuring, clinically trustworthy, and elegantly restrained.

## 2. Visual Identity & UI
### 2.1 Aesthetic Foundation
- **Generous Negative Space:** Prioritize "air" around all elements to reduce visual noise and emphasize a single-task focus.
- **Soft, Rounded Geometry:** Use refined corner radii and organic shapes to make the interface feel warm, approachable, and humanly centered.
- **Subtle, Depth-Driven Layers:** Employ light shadows and layered surfaces to create structure and a sense of premium "tactility" without feeling heavy or corporate.

### 2.2 Typography and Rhythm
- **Vertical Rhythm:** Apply a strict spacing system (e.g., 8px base) to create a sense of order, professional stability, and structural calm.
- **Typographic Hierarchy:** Use clear contrast between headlines (e.g., Manrope/Instrument Sans) and UI/Body text (e.g., Inter/Geist) to make the primary intention of each screen instantly obvious.
- **Airy Tracking:** Apply subtly increased letter-spacing to headlines and key UI elements to make the interface feel breathable, contemporary, and emotionally light.

## 3. Interaction Design
### 3.1 Meditative Onboarding
- **Progressive Reveal:** Show only one question or decision at a time to prevent cognitive overload and maintain a "zero-learning-curve" experience.
- **Reassuring Transitions:** Use soft, intentional animations and transitions to guide the user's eye and create a sense of continuity.
- **Immediate Value Feedback:** Every action should result in a clear, positive outcome (e.g., "We've found 3 free slots on Monday") to build confidence and reward progress.

## 4. Emotional Signature
- **Pallette Direction:** A "serene and organic" base of warm whites and off-whites, accented by sage greens and soft slate blues to subcommunicate growth, stability, and clinical-grade trust.
